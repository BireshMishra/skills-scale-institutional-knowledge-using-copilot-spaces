# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Quality Assurance Lead (QA Lead)

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads define and enforce testing standards across the project lifecycle. They coordinate manual and automated QA efforts, maintain test plans, and ensure quality gates are met before releases.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain testing standards and QA processes
- Coordinate manual and automated QA efforts across sprints
- Maintain test plans, test cases, and QA reports
- Handle defect triage and prioritization with Project Managers
- Validate that acceptance criteria are testable and complete
- Ensure security scanning and compliance checks are integrated in CI
- Sign off on release quality gates

### Goals
- Minimize production defects and customer-impacting issues
- Maintain high test coverage and automation rates
- Enable fast, confident releases through robust quality processes
- Provide clear quality metrics and visibility to stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report on QA progress and blockers
- Weekly syncs with Project Managers for release readiness reviews
- Collaboration with Developers on test coverage and automation
- Quality reports and defect metrics in sprint reviews

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers translate product requirements into user flows, wireframes, and prototypes. They conduct usability testing and advocate for accessibility and user-centered design throughout the development process.

### Responsibilities
- Create user flows, wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research and usability testing sessions
- Validate designs with Product Managers and stakeholders
- Ensure accessibility standards (WCAG) are met
- Collaborate with Developers on implementation feasibility
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Participate in sprint reviews to validate implemented features

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction metrics
- Ensure design consistency across features
- Advocate for user needs throughout the product lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Planning sessions with Product Managers to align on requirements
- Design reviews and critiques with stakeholders
- Collaboration with Developers during implementation
- Usability testing sessions with users and stakeholders
- Sprint demos to validate design implementation

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons facilitate regular communication between the project team and external stakeholders. They ensure feedback loops are timely and actionable, track approvals, and align on business priorities.

### Responsibilities
- Identify stakeholder groups and their communication needs
- Coordinate regular updates and status reports to stakeholders
- Gather and synthesize stakeholder feedback for the team
- Track approvals, sign-offs, and decision gates
- Escalate stakeholder concerns to Project and Product Managers
- Maintain stakeholder maps and communication plans
- Bridge communication between technical teams and business units

### Goals
- Ensure stakeholder alignment and minimize surprises
- Accelerate decision-making through proactive communication
- Maintain stakeholder satisfaction and trust
- Reduce escalations by addressing concerns early

### Typical Communication
- Weekly or bi-weekly stakeholder update meetings
- Monthly stakeholder newsletters or status reports
- Ad-hoc escalations and decision requests
- Coordination with Project Managers on risk and status
- Feedback sessions with Product Managers on priorities

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers automate CI/CD pipelines, manage infrastructure as code, and monitor system reliability and performance post-release. They implement deployment strategies and ensure rollback procedures are in place.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Manage infrastructure as code (IaC) and environment provisioning
- Implement deployment strategies (blue-green, canary, rolling)
- Monitor system reliability, performance, and observability
- Ensure security scanning is integrated in build pipelines
- Document and test rollback and disaster recovery procedures
- Collaborate on release planning and deployment windows

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and repeatable deployments
- Maintain high system reliability and uptime
- Reduce deployment risk through automation and observability
- Accelerate feedback loops with monitoring and alerts

### Typical Communication
- Collaboration with Developers on release readiness and CI/CD
- Weekly syncs with Project Managers for deployment planning
- Coordination with QA Lead on test environment provisioning
- Incident response and post-mortems for production issues
- Infrastructure and security updates in team meetings
